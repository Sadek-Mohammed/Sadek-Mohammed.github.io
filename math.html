My paper

Summary

The paper starts with a quick look into the known formula of the sum of the elements in the rows of the Pascal's triangle (A triangle that is formed by making each element the result of adding the two elements above it with putting 1s on diagonals) for the sake of introducing a generalized formula for any triangle that is formed the same way as Pascal's triangle but with sets other than 1s on the diagonals. Next, the paper introduces the pracitcal use of the invented formula in the process of proving formulas that were previously set by another researcher and went unproved. After that, the paper introduces other formulas for the sum of rows of triangles, where each entry is the result of adding 4, 6, or 8 elements above it with any set on the diagonals. Finally, by noticing the similarity of the generalized formula for 2, 4, 6, and 8 triangles, I could propose a formula that works for any Pascal-like (Pascalized) triangle where each entry is the result of adding any number of entries from the above row with any set on the diagonals, inviting other researches to find a formal proof for it.
