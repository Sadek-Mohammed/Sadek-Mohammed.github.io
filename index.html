Math Passion

Brief

I got an "A" in my Grade 11 Math curriculum, putting me beside the top maths students in my shcool. Away from the intense Math curriculum that I take at school, I did research through the Pioneer Academics program with Prof. Greg Dresden of W&Lee university for 2 months. In addition to my experience with the program, I experience Math with each and every Compeitive Coding problem I solve. That's because informatics problems almost always depend on mathematical analysis, a formula, or a sort of logical thinking, which is what I love Math (or any sort of thinking motive) for.

Paper

The paper starts with a quick look into the known formula of the sum of the elements in the rows of the Pascal's triangle (A triangle that is formed by making each element the result of adding the two elements above it with putting 1s on diagonals) for the sake of introducing a generalized formula for any triangle that is formed the same way as Pascal's triangle but with sets other than 1s on the diagonals. Next, the paper introduces the pracitcal use of the invented formula in the process of proving some conjectures (formulas that were previously set by another researcher and went unproved). After that, the paper introduces other formulas for the sum of rows of triangles, where each entry is the result of adding 4, 6, or 8 elements above it with any set on the diagonals. Finally, by noticing the similarity of the generalized formula for 2, 4, 6, and 8 triangles, I could propose a conjecture that works for any Pascal-like (Pascalized) triangle where each entry is the result of adding any number of entries from the above row with any set on the diagonals, inviting other researches to find a formal proof for it.

Grade 11, 2nd Semester Capstone Project | Gentian Blossom

Brief

As any of the school's Capstone Projects, the project focues on solving one of the 11 grand challenges of Egypt, and out of the those choices, the project's requirements focused more on industry. "Utilizing Industrial Processes using a Feedback Control Mechanis coupled with either waste material or green energy" made the extremely specific design challenge (file with constraints and project's requirements put by the Egyptian Ministry of Education). In short, the challenge focused some points. From the millions of ways to improve industry, the project specified the necessity of using a feedback control mechanism, which is an automated system that controls the quality of the product and makes decisions on whether the product was ideal or not. In addition to boosting industry, the project had the aim of helping to sustain the environment by restricting the use of either waste materials or green energy.

Emotions

As a member of my community, I noticed the extent to which the country financially suffers from the poor industrial processes: Egypt's gross domestic product harshly decreased, unemployment rate exponentially increased, and export-to-import ratio requests urgent help. That encouraged me to choose a multi-purpose industry. In addition to the decrease in the number of industries, the quality of the products is really bad Improving industry, limiting unemployment, and sustaining environment make up a satisfying mix for a devloping country. That's why I was sure that the feedback control mechanism I would use will work on making the quality better. Finally, my team preferred working on a green energy source to make the industrial process feasible - even in the harshest times.

Description

My father, a pharmacist, was usually laughing about the Gentian Violet (GV) solution, an anti-microbial solution. He used to say that it is a gram of powder + some goofy amount water, and that was enough for pharmaceutical companies to sell 25mL for ~10 EGP, which was too high in my father's Point of view. From the early beginnings of the project, I thought of that industry as our perfect solution: the only component was accessible and controllable. Another reason is that Gentian Violet is used in other industries, too, as a dye. My team proposed the feedback control mechanism for Gentian Violet to depend on pH. If the gentian violet concentration becomes too much, the solution turns acidic, and dilation is needed to adapt. Otherwise, on lower concentrations, the soution is basic/neutral, and adding more GV is required. The arduino, two pumps (one for water & the other for GV), and pH sensor are required for controlling the whole process. Finally, solar panels were added to provide the prototype with a green source of energy.

Role

Alongside doing research and writing, the whole team searches, proposes, and thinks of ideas. Of the gathered ideas, one is chosen by the whole team. Luckily, mine idea was chosen because it was a bit specific and serves multiple industries. Anyways, after finding the idea, the prototype needed to be implemented. As the main software enthusiast, I did all of the codes, which complemented Hamdy (the tallest person in the exhibition idea)'s circuits passion, finishing the fully-fledged website. After all, I worked with my team in both laboratory experiments and preparing for the main exhibition - in front of university professors.

Grade 11, 1st Semester Capstone Project | Slope Desalinator

Brief

As any of the school's Capstone Projects, the project focues on solving one of the 11 grand challenges of Egypt, and out of the those choices, the project's requirements focused AGAIN on Clean Water Resources. "How can Egypt increase the sources of water for domestic, agriculture, or industries in a safe and environmentally responsible manner?" made the extremely specific design challenge (file with constraints and project's requirements put by the Egyptian Ministry of Education). In short, the challenge focused some points. Among the 3 main uses of water (agricultural, domestic, and industrial) uses, one had to be chosen. After finding the suitable field, the project shall focus on increasing the sources of water for that specific field but in a way that is safe & sustains environment, matching the Egyptian 2030 vision.

Emotions

Egypt has always been a country of agriculture, and I cannot forget my grandfather's stories about the best banana kilogram that he bought immediately after the 1973 war (I can confidently claim that each and every Egyptian can claim to ). That encouraged my team to work on utilizing the sources of water for agriculture. For a team, working on agriculture was a convenient goal: agriculture usually accepts all sorts of water, unless the water's pH isn't suitable for the specific field or TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) was too high that it means the presence of heavy metals or too low that it refers to the absence of salts. Among the various types of water that could be utilized (domestic, rainwater, underground water, and others), my team found rainwater more convenient as it was the focus of the 10 th Grade challenge.

Science

The project depended on a container that holds waiter and a heater. The project's idea was simple: we need to heat water and, then, condense the water again to get the pure water particles to condense together (without pollutants) in the collecting place, returning to the liquid water state. That desalinator has the advantage of using a slope to fasten the movement of particles towards the collection place. A brilliant addition to the protoype was adding the aluminium sulphate (alum) powder, which is a cheap desalinator that forms easily-segregated compounds with pollutants - easing the process of water desalination.

Role

As usual, much research & writing was needed, especially as our team consisted of 3 members (not 4 as most teams), giving me more work to do. In addition, the scientific base of the protoype needed much research and adjustements as it contained concepts water desalinators, heat, energy, and other multi-disciplinary concepts. Since the protoype consisted of zero software, my team had to go to multiple vendors. Finally, I strengthened the design skills I learned the previous years by handling the portfolio's design.

Grade 11, 1st Semester Capstone Project | Hexa Project

Brief

As any of the school's Capstone Projects, the project focues on solving one of the 11 grand challenges of Egypt, and out of the those choices, the project's requirements focused more on Clean Water Resources. "How can we make the best use of rainfalls? Which are the best locations in Egypt in order to maximize system efficiency?" made the extremely specific design challenge (file with constraints and project's requirements put by the Egyptian Ministry of Education - MOE). As the first capstone project in my cohort's journey at our STEM school, the MOE took care of making the challenge extremely simple in order to help us focus more on gaining the basic but necessary knowledge of research, academic writing, and presenting along with adapting to the school's boarding, harsh nature and advanced curriclum. The challenges ordered us to choose a way, place, and a design that collects as much as water as possible and focused on the use of Wooden Tongue Depressors. as the main building material.

Emotions

Once entering the school, my team studied the scientific method & history of science in the physics and chemistry sessions. One of the main points that the origin of science depended upon was putting natural phenomena (God's creations) into action in scientific breakthroughs. Our team took the compact shape of beehives as a model for our protoype to follow as it stores the most honey per the provided area. In addition, pushed by the diminshed, flooded homes of the thousands of Egyptians living on the Northern Coast area, we recommended the use of the prototype there to utilize the 200 mm/yr of rainfall (considered the highest in Egypt).

Workflow

The most interesting part about the project was the workflow of the team. The team was all new to scientific research, and two of us went to schools with all-arabic curriculum before getting to my STEM school. In addition, COVID-19, along with inexperience, forced the team into communication problems for the first month. We faced problems while doing everything from planning to executing. Although teams in the 10th grade consist of 5 members, everyone's poor experience made everyone have double responsibilities. with time, all of us utlilized their resarch, writing, and planning experience into better outcomes, and I participated in all of the group's activities (thinking, discussions, testing the protoype, and offline gathering instead of the cancelled school meetings). I could also design our poster and learned how to present in an engaging way for the Exhibition Day.

Science

Since we were obliged to use the Wooden Tongue Depressors, we had to consider all of the possibilities of wood glue, and we chose the PolyVinyl Acetate (PVA) glue due to its strength, cheap price, and water resistance. The prototype was prepared by gluing the Tongue Depressors together, cutting the trapezoidal faces of the prototype, and gluing them together into the beehive shape. The epoxy resin was our coating material due to its strength against various weather conditions. It's important to avoid neglecting the amount of geometry used to calculate the dimensions of that trapezoidal pyramid prototype and the amount of chemistry needed to understand the way glues work with wood. All of those different sciences resulted in learning enough information for our exhibition.

Moonshoot #Beapirate Challenge | Braille Brains

Brief

This challenge is a competition for creative minds, ages between 14 and 19. The 2021 challenge, in which my team and I participated, contained about 800 young changemakers from 80+ countries, forming 65 teams. The competition was mainly formed as a platform to give youth mentoring and guide them into making innovate ideas that do really make change. My team and I got the 2 nd place for an idea that helps people with deaf-blindness, where we were a group of 6 innovators from 4 countries: Egypt, Germany, India, and Indonesia, and all of the collaboration was handled via Zoom Video Software, presentation via Canva, Coding via Visual Studio Code.

Emotions

Most of the team members came from poor communities that are extremely affected by any slight changes in finances, dollar exchange rate, and, even, governmental loans. In those countries, work of as many family members as possible and as few expenses as possible are REALLY mandatory. If the family has a person that is deaf or blind, the whole family may suffer: less income from the sick person & more expenses for their cure. In addition to financial hardships, deaf OR blind people suffer from many social problems due to being unable to contact with their peers, so what about being deaf AND blind? That thought inspired the project makers to help the 15 million people world wide that suffer from that disease.

MODEL

Since the competition is online, the project wasn't required to be made in a hardware form. The first sort of modelling was the idea canvas. Then, the project was modelled as being a braille-text and speech-braille translator: when someone speaks a message, the message is converted into braille (language for blind people) text. On the other hand, there is a braille keyboard that converts braille text to either speech or sends messages as a response to the recepient. During the period between the idea canvas and the final pitch, more decisions on the project's design were disucssed, starting from the method of translating the braille text to the other physical design parts. For starters, the keyboard was designed in a smaller form with fewere number of keys, the use of solar panels was finally agreed upon (became lighter to ease transportation), and, finally, the cloud services that will help the voice-to-braille and vice versa automatically were carefully chosen. The result of plans on the funding, preparations, and diagrams were added to the presentation.

WORKFLOW

As the top tech enthusiast in the group, I was responsible for managing each and every technical aspect of the project. From building the website in a single day to managing the smallest details of the project's workflow. For the final pitch, I had to prepare for possible questions like choosing the APIs (automated services that are publicaly accessible) and cloud-based services that will manage text-to-braille conversion, how will the device communicate, and others. Finally, I extensively contributed to the research process while trying to find the idea, how it will work, and enhancements.
