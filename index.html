The October Maths Circle Competitions (OMCC)

Brief

A website for OMCC, the school's main Math extracurricular, where a group of math enthusiasts publish Math handouts that discuss relatively hard concepts such as introductory calculus and precalculus. The OMCC has the main goal of improving the students' problem solving skills and Math capapbilities. Throughout the summer, OMCC's stuff have organized a Calculus Camp that inspires the beginners to pursue Math.

Inspiration

I had three main causes to join. The first is that I have personally seen people who suffered from the huge jump between the normal Math classes and the Calculus ones, so I was encouraged to decrease the severe suffering that the next calculus learners will face. The second reason is that through solving informatics problems, I have sensed the importance of problem-solving skills which pushes me forward into introducing other people to the refreshing feeling of being able to solve problems & conquer formulas. The third reason was that the period of working on that project conincided with my first days with my Math combinatorics research paper, and I really loved it, so I wanted to work beside a group of math enthusiasts and have a closer look into their Calculus concepts. All of those reasons surged me into putting my tech experience to boost their workflow.

Role

I had the role of building the website along with my 2 peers. Building the website consisted of many parts: doing extensive edits into a pre-existing template, implementing advanced functions such as contact forms and LATEX-integrated questions, and developing the backend temporary exam gate. The backend gate was a sort of a database that stores the examinees, their emails, and scores. In addition, it stores the exam questions and corrects them using reference correct answers. Throughout all of those endeavors, I had to collaborate with my friends using zoom & other communication softwares as well as using version control websites such as Github.

Grade 11, 2nd Semester Capstone Project | Gentian Blossom
What is the project? As any of the school's Capstone Projects, the project focues on solving one of the 11 grand challenges of Egypt, and out of the those choices, the project's requirements focused more on industry. "Utilizing Industrial Processes using a Feedback Control Mechanis coupled with either waste material or green energy" made the extremely specific desing challenge. From the millions of ways to improve industry, the project specified the necessity of using a feedback control mechanism, which is an automated system that controls the quality of the product and makes decisions on whether the product was ideal or not. In addition to boosting industry, the project had the aim of helping to sustain the environment by restricting the use of either waste materials or green energy.
What drove me to work? As a member of my community, I noticed the extent to which the country financially suffers from the poor industrial processes: Egypt's gross domestic product harshly decreased, unemployment rate exponentially increased, and export-to-import ratio requests urgent help. In addition to the decrease in the number of industries, the quality of the products is really bad Improving industry, limiting unemployment, and sustaining environment make up a satisfying mix for a devloping country.
What was the workflow and my role? I had the role of building the website along with my 2 peers. The backend gate was a sort of a database that stores the examinees, their emails, and scores. In addition, it stores the exam questions and corrects them using reference correct answers.
